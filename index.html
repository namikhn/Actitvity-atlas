Four ready-made trips

Loose day- or weekend-plans matched to who's in the back seat.

With a baby (0–2)

Gentle, shaded, stroller-first

  1. ZOO Planckendael — wide flat paths, shade, feeding times.
  2. Bokrijk — open-air museum with soft-grass playfields.
  3. De Haan beach — gentle dunes, tram at the door.
  4. Rainy swap: Aquatopia — dark, warm, mesmerising tanks.
With a toddler (2–5)

Characters, animals, short attention spans

  1. Plopsaland De Panne — indoor Mayaland beats any weather.
  2. Pairi Daiza — split over two days; playgrounds throughout.
  3. Boudewijn Seapark — small rides plus a dolphin show.
  4. Rainy swap: Plopsa Indoor Hasselt — a whole park under a roof.
Primary school (6–12)

Coasters, caves and curious minds

  1. Walibi Belgium — mid-size coasters before the big ones.
  2. Caves of Han — tram, guided cave walk, wolves.
  3. Technopolis — hundreds of hands-on science stations.
  4. Euro Space Center — rockets and moon-walk simulators.
Teenagers (13–18)

Adrenaline and bragging rights

  1. Walibi — Kondaa, the tallest, fastest coaster in Benelux.
  2. Adventure Valley Durbuy — high ropes, kayaking, via ferrata.
  3. Bobbejaanland — Typhoon and the Fury launch coaster.
  4. Down day: kayak the Lesse, or the High Fens on foot.

How to read this atlas

Colour = region. Gold is Flanders, red is Wallonia, teal is Brussels — the coding runs through the pills and the coloured edge of every card, so you can scan by region at a glance.

The photos. Each card tries to load a freely-licensed image from Wikipedia; where none exists (smaller or newer venues), a category illustration stands in. If a photo ever looks wrong for a place, that's a Wikipedia title mismatch — tell me and I'll pin the right one.

What "rating" means. A curated family-suitability score (variety for mixed ages, value, pram-ease, weather resilience) — a starting opinion, not an average of public reviews.
